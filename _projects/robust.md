When models are uncertain and decisions are safety-critical, standard expected-value
optimization is insufficient. This project develops **risk-aware decision making**
frameworks using CVaR-based objectives, chance constraints, and distributionally
robust optimization for scientific applications.

We proposed distributionally robust BOED using ambiguity sets {% cite go2022robust %},
developed optimal design methodologies for epidemiological control under uncertainty,
and studied robust active learning for neural PDE solvers {% cite wang2025plugandplay %}.

Methods include conditional value-at-risk (CVaR), ambiguity sets, chance constraints,
and distributionally robust optimization combined with Bayesian inference and surrogate
models. Applications span epidemiological control, power systems, and pharmacokinetics.
